# CKSource Applied AI Cover Letter Design

## Goal

Adapt the cover letter in this repository for the `Principal Applied AI Engineer` role at `CKSource` so it reads as a concrete, technical application focused on:

- applied AI delivery
- end-to-end systems design
- pragmatic engineering judgment
- hands-on implementation in `Node.js / TypeScript` environments

The result should sound technically credible and aligned with the job description, without overstating prior experience.

## Source Context

The target role is the `Principal Applied AI Engineer` offer published by `CKSource` on `Just Join IT` and reviewed on `2026-04-04`.

The job description emphasizes:

- solving high-impact problems across products and internal systems with AI and automation
- moving from problem definition to architecture to implementation
- building multi-step and agentic workflows
- improving how software is built internally through AI-assisted development systems
- making sound decisions around model choice, orchestration, latency, cost, reliability, security, and maintainability
- strong backend and systems design skills
- preference for `TypeScript` and `Node.js`

## Candidate Positioning

The approved positioning is a hybrid of:

1. `AI / agentic workflows`
2. `system design + delivery`

The tone should remain `concrete and technical`, not aspirational or marketing-heavy.

The letter should present Wojciech as a senior hands-on engineer who already uses AI in practical delivery work and who can design, build, and operationalize AI-enabled systems rather than only prototype them.

## Core Narrative

### 1. Lead with applied AI ownership

The opening paragraph should establish immediate fit for the role:

- direct application to `Principal Applied AI Engineer`
- current work spans `Lexense.ai`, QA leadership, and engineering consulting
- AI is positioned as part of day-to-day engineering work, not as a side topic
- emphasis on moving from real problem statements to working systems

This section should avoid generic claims such as "I am passionate about AI."

### 2. Use two evidence blocks

The body should rely on two specific evidence groups.

#### `Lexense.ai`

This should demonstrate:

- architecture ownership of an AI-native legal platform
- backend and knowledge-system design around `Node.js / NestJS`
- practical use of `OpenAI`, `RAG`, `OCR`, document analysis, legal search, and conversational or voice workflows
- security-first handling of sensitive data
- production concerns such as self-hosting, observability, and CI/CD

This is the primary proof that the candidate builds AI-enabled product systems end to end.

#### `Efectivo / Humanit Group`

This should demonstrate:

- design of `LLM`-powered agents for test automation tasks
- creation of `LLM + RAG` product documentation / knowledge base workflows
- work across QA, CI/CD, observability, and cross-team delivery
- practical internal AI enablement rather than research experimentation

This is the primary proof that the candidate applies AI to internal engineering workflows, which is a direct match to the CKSource role.

### 3. Close on engineering judgment and influence

The final paragraph should explicitly connect the prior experience to what CKSource needs:

- pragmatic use of AI where it creates real value
- ability to make trade-offs across architecture, reliability, maintainability, and delivery speed
- comfort working across product, engineering, and business context
- ability to help teams adopt AI-assisted workflows sensibly

The close should sound grounded and delivery-oriented rather than visionary.

## Content Structure

The final letter should use three compact paragraphs:

1. `Fit and role framing`
2. `Evidence from Lexense and Efectivo`
3. `Why this matches CKSource`

The total length should remain close to the existing tailored cover letters in the repository. It should be concise enough to fit naturally in the current template without turning into a project summary.

## Messaging Constraints

### Include

- `applied AI`
- `agentic workflows` or equivalent wording
- `Node.js / TypeScript` alignment
- end-to-end system design and implementation
- production mindset: security, observability, CI/CD, maintainability
- cross-functional collaboration

### Avoid

- inflated "principal" language unsupported by evidence
- vague enthusiasm statements
- generic soft-skill filler
- research-oriented framing
- large stack dumps

## Drafting Guidance

The wording should make clear that:

- the candidate has already built AI-native product capabilities
- the candidate has already used LLM-based tooling to improve internal engineering workflows
- the candidate is comfortable in backend-heavy and operationally real environments
- the candidate's strongest differentiator is combining AI implementation with systems thinking and delivery discipline

The wording should also stay honest about the profile:

- it should not imply years of specialization in formal ML research
- it should position the candidate as an applied engineering builder

## Acceptance Criteria

- The letter clearly targets `CKSource` and the `Principal Applied AI Engineer` role.
- The dominant narrative is `applied AI + systems delivery`.
- `Lexense.ai` and `Efectivo / Humanit Group` provide the main supporting evidence.
- `Node.js / TypeScript` alignment is visible.
- AI-assisted internal engineering workflows are explicitly mentioned.
- The tone is technical, direct, and credible.
- The final text remains short enough for the current LaTeX cover letter format.

## Out of Scope

- Rewriting the CV itself
- Inflating the profile into a research-scientist narrative
- Adding new experience claims not already supported by repository materials
- Changing the visual design of the cover letter template
